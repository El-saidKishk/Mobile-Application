Food Groups

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are compounds made up of hydrogen, carbon and oxygen, they are divided into simple carbohydrates (like sugars) and complex carbohydrates (such as starch). Simple carbohydrates are simple sugar molecules like glucose, fructose and sucrose, they are found in food like fruit, chocolate and milk. Complex carbohydrates (or starch) are composed of a long chain of sugars joined together and are found in bread, rice, pasta and potatoes.

Proteins

Proteins

Proteins are molecules made up of amino acids and are the building blocks of the body's structure. They are needed for the function and regulation of organs. There are 20 types of amino acids that can be combined to make different proteins. Proteins are found in foods such as meat, eggs, fish, nuts and milk.

Fats

Fats

Fats are a type of nutrient that gives your body energy, it also helps you absorb certain vitamins like vitamin A,D,E and K which are fat-souble vitamins. There are 2 main types of fats saturated fats and unsaturated fats, saturated fats are what raise your cholesterol level and are found in cream, butter, cheese, whole milk and ice cream. Unsaturated fats are found in vegetables.

Fruit and Vegetables

Fruit and Vegetables

Fruit and vegetables are rich in antioxidabts which are compounds that scavenge and neutralise free radicals. They also contain folic acid which decreases the amount of homocysteine in the blood which is substance that is a risk factor for coronary heart disease.

Dairy Products

Dairy Products

Dairy products are great source of protein, calcium, vitamin D and other healthy nutrients. These nutrients are involved with bone health, cardiovascular disease and other conditions. The calcium, phosphorus and vitamin D found in dairy products help with bone building and the high amount of potassium helps lower blood pressure.
